Kazuma's Marketing Empire (Don't Let Them Repo The Urus)
Statement

After blowing three Topstep funded accounts, Kazuma, Aqua, Megumin, and Darkness pooled their remaining payout to lease a Lamborghini Urus together for content. The plan: funnel clout across their marketing channels and sell enough subscriptions of their paid course "The Isekai Trading Matrix ($125 monthly)" on Whop before the repo man shows up.

Their marketing network has n platforms and m bidirectional collab deals. Each deal between platforms u and v costs c coins to activate -- cross-promotions aren't free. They start at platform 1 (their broke Discord server) and need to reach platform n (Course Sales). Platforms are interconnected in a web of clout with possible cycles -- a collab deal can be revisited, but that would just waste money.

Kazuma has exactly B coins left. Find the minimum marketing cost to reach Course Sales. If it is impossible to reach or the minimum cost exceeds B, print -1. Aqua will cry either way.

Input

The first line contains a single integer t (1 ≤ t ≤ 104) -- the number of test cases.

Each test case begins with three integers n, m, and B. The next m lines each contain three integers u, v, c describing a bidirectional edge between u and v with cost c.

Output

For each test case, print the minimum cost to travel from platform 1 to platform n, or -1 if it is impossible or exceeds B.

Constraints
1 ≤ t ≤ 104
2 ≤ n ≤ 2×105, 1 ≤ m ≤ 2×105
1 ≤ B, c ≤ 109
Graph may contain cycles. All edge weights are positive.
Sum of n and m across all test cases ≤ 2×105
Sample I/O
Sample 1
Input
2 5 6 20 1 2 5 1 3 10 2 4 3 2 5 15 3 4 2 4 5 7 3 2 5 1 2 3 2 3 10
Output
15 -1
Test 1: path 1 to 2 to 4 to 5 costs 5+3+7 = 15. Urus safe. Test 2: cheapest path costs 13, which exceeds B = 5. Repo man wins.
